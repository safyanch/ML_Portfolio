Turn 2
Student: Can you explain what overfitting means?
Tutor:   Hello Ahmad. Overfitting occurs when a model is too complex and learns the noise or randomness in the training data, instead of the underlying patterns. As a result, the model performs well on the training data but poorly on new, unseen data, leading to a high error rate. This is because the model has become too specialized in memorizing the training data, rather than generalizing to the larger population.
--------------------------------------------------

Turn 3
Student: What techniques can prevent the problem you just explained?
Tutor:   Hello Ahmad. To prevent overfitting, some common techniques are:

1. **Regularization**: Adding a penalty term to the loss function to discourage large weights.
2. **Data augmentation**: Increasing the size of the training dataset by applying transformations (e.g., rotation, scaling) to existing data.
3. **Early stopping**: Stopping training when the model's performance on the validation set starts to degrade.
4. **Cross-validation**: Training and evaluating the model on multiple subsets of the data to avoid overfitting to a single subset.
5. **Decreasing model complexity**: Using simpler models or fewer parameters to avoid overfitting.
